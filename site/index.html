Methodology & Caveats

Data Source

Contract awards from USASpending.gov, queried via three layered searches:

  1. All contracts with PSC code F110 ("Development of EIS/EA, Technical Analysis/Environmental Audits")
  2. Keyword "environmental impact statement" across all PSC codes
  3. Keyword "environmental assessment" filtered to NAICS 541620 (Environmental Consulting Services)

Results are deduplicated and classified as EIS or EA based on description text matching.

What This Data Shows

Federal agency expenditures on contractors for NEPA document preparation.

What This Data Does Not Show

  • Internal agency staff costs — only contractor payments are captured.
  • Categorical Exclusions (CEs) — rarely involve standalone contracts; typically done in-house.
  • Applicant-funded NEPA work — when project proponents hire their own NEPA consultants (common for FERC-jurisdictional energy projects), those costs are private transactions invisible to procurement data.
  • Micro-purchases — contracts below the $15,000 threshold are not reported to FPDS.

Classification Accuracy

EIS/EA classification uses regex matching on contract descriptions. Some contracts cover multiple document types or general NEPA support. The "NEPA Support" category captures contracts coded under PSC F110 that don't mention a specific document type. Manual overrides can correct misclassifications.

Award Amount

The "Award Amount" is the total obligated amount, which may include contract modifications and may not equal the final cost. For IDIQ/BPA task orders, the amount reflects the individual task order, not the parent contract ceiling.
